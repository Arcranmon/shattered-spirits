### Character Equipment
Characters need to carry weapons, armor, food, and various other supplies to survive in the Shattered World. While traveling light carries many benefits, characters that travel without food, bandages, antidotes, and other supplies may find their journey cut uncomfortably short.

&nbsp;

### Load
Inventory in Shattered Spirits is abstracted away to _Load_. Load is a combination of the size and weight of an object. As a general rule, an item the size of a large knife or Potion is a single _Slot_, one-handed weapons and tools are 2 _Slots_, and two-handed weapons and Equipment are 3 _Slots_.

&nbsp;
A character's inventory is divided up into their _Worn_ and _Packed_ items. A _Worn_ Item is easily accessible, and can be used in high-pressure or combat situations with no additional risks, while _Packed_ items are in a pack, secured, or otherwise inaccessible and require more effort to access. Some items, like Armor, must be in a _Worn_ slot to gain any benefit.

&nbsp;

Characters track _Worn_ and _Packed_ equipment separately. Characters may carry up to 12 _Slots_ of Packed Equipment, and may carry a variable amount of _Worn_ equipment, which impacts their _Encumbrance_.

&nbsp;

### Encumbrance
Depending on how many equipment slots, characters will be Lightly, Moderately, or Heavily Encumbered. Characters with a Lightly Encumbered characters gain the _Traveling Light_ and _Unencumbered_ Abilities, while those with a Heavily Encumbered gain _Traveling Heavy_ and _Encumbered_. Moderately Encumbered does not provide any advantages or penalties.

&nbsp;

Characters with a _Worn_ _Load_ of 5 or less have a _Light_ _Load_, 10 or less have a _Medium_, and 15 or less have a _Heavy_. Characters may not travel with a _Load_ greater than 15.
&nbsp;

### Equipment in Combat
In combat, characters' equipment is defined by what they have _Worn_ and what they have _Wielded_.
&nbsp;

_Worn_ items are anything in a character's _Worn_ slots. A character's _Armor_, any consumable items, and any _Weapons_ stowed on their consumables are common examples of _Worn_ items.
&nbsp;

_Wielded_ items, on the other hand, are items that characters actively are using, typically by holding it in a hand. One-Handed items require a single hand to _Wield_, while Two-Handed items require both.
&nbsp;

If a character does not have an item _Wielded_ in a hand, that hand is considered an _Open Hand_.
&nbsp;

**Equipment and Open Hands:** In some cases Equipment must be _Wielded_ in two hands or gets additional benefits when _Wielded_ in such a way. In cases such as this, when a character is _Wielding_ a single piece of Equipment, they may choose on each _Turn_ if they are _Wielding_ it in one or both hands, but may not use it unless it is _Wielded_ in the appropriate number of hands.
&nbsp;
