### The World Itself
Within the Shattered World, elements do not always act according to their standard rules. Stones can float, fire can spontaneously appear, and air existing is not a guarantee. Weather is more intense, resources are frequently sparse, and simple eking out another day of survival is a difficult task. The world is a wild, dangerous place, with towns acting as small pockets of shelter, security, and relative normalcy.
&nbsp;
### Spirits
In addition to the above, nearly everything in the world possesses its own will, however small. The simple act of gathering firewood can potentially anger wood spirits, turning a mundane task in a dangerous encounter. People learn from a young age to watch for the signs of spirits and be wary of any source of elements, and many children learn the hard way that a stray puddle could be a water spirit resting. While not an enemy, spirits are almost always wild, and always dangerous, even moreso when a daemon is involved.
&nbsp;
### Spirit Beasts
Much how humans are linked to a spirit, a similar effect can happen with wild animals. While they don't manifest a physical spirit, they still gain control over that element, and typically exhibit far greater raw power than a human would. These traits seem to be able to passed on from generation to generation, leading to incredibly powerful animals and other beasts. Predators imbued with the strength of the earth or hawks capable of breathing fire exist in the world, and livestock born with spirit powers are both a powerful asset and dangerous tool. While not a primary danger, many areas of the world are so infested with spirit beasts as to be basically impassable.
&nbsp;
### Locusts
A plague and anomaly within the world, locusts seem to be the anathema to spirits. They control no elements and are completely natural, but consume with a voracious appetite. When moving into a new area they will establish a safe lair for their queen then begin consuming the local fauna, bringing samples of spirits and creatures alike back to their queen. With each clutch of eggs the colony of locusts grows more and more specialized and more and more capable of dealing with local threats. If a colony isn't wiped out by an initial assault, a completely different approach is recommended for future encounters. A locust colony, left unchecked, can cause irreparable harm to an area in a matter of weeks.
&nbsp;
Beyond their rapid reproduction speed, ability to rapidly iterate new traits in subsequent generations and propensity to consume, locusts also have a peculiar venom. Typically, poisons only affect humans, animals, and other like creatures, but whatever locusts have affects spirits and creatures alike. It is incredibly difficult to extract; they have multiple chambers of slightly different venoms, none of which seem to be as potent as what they inject. Alchemists theorize that whatever is injected is mixed the instant before injection, and pay handsomely for any recovered samples.
&nbsp;
### The Underworld
The colloquial name for the ruins of the old world buried beneath the ground, The Underworld is far easier to avoid than the other hazards, but no less dangerous. The few paths to The Underworld seem to have been fortified and made as impassable as possible with elaborate mazes, deadly traps, and as much confusing architecture as possible. In the deeper levels there are a host of riches; ancient machines, texts that, while fragile, carry secrets of the old world, and rare and precious materials. There are rumors of strange spirits that are common within the depths, some hybrid of fire, water, and metal that are extremely territorial and hostile to outsiders, although most treat those as an old wives' tale from delvers.
### Humanity
While the nature of Guardian Spirits and the like makes large-scale conflicts rare, bandits and raiders are still a threat within the Shattered World. They are uncommon, but anyone able to survive in the wilds for an extended period is extremely capable and dangerous.
&nbsp;
Despite Guardian Spirits, there are those that are willing to make moves on towns; typically, these are raiding strikes, attempting to get in and out before the spirit can respond but, among the more clever and patient, long-running plots to turn a spirit against their people and take the remains of a town are possible, if difficult, to execute.
