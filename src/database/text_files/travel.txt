### Traveling in the Shattered World
The Shattered World is a beautiful, wild, dangerous place, and travelers leaving the safety of their town or village must be well-prepared. Besides the obvious dangers of hunger, thirst, weather, and dangerous beasts, the Shattered World carries with it other, greater dangers, like impossible weather, territorial spirits and, of course, areas still ravaged by the Shattering itself.
&nbsp; 

### The Phases of a Journey
Play in Shattered Spirits is structured around the phases of a Journey. Each Journey represents a trip away from home, and is where the bulk of gameplay comes in.

&nbsp;

Before beginning a Journey, characters first have to find why they're going on a Journey! Common motivations are to find a rare resource or medicine, find a piece of lost knowledge, or to beg a boon from a distant spirit. Early on these motivations will likely be more concrete but, as the players explore the world and make bonds with NPCs, they may come up with journeys that they wish to attempt.

&nbsp; 

Once a goal and destination are decided, characters will then break their Journey down into Legs, which represent the primary stops in the Journey. Some shorter Journeys may only have a single Leg (journey origin to the final destination), but longer Journeys will frequently be comprised of multiple Legs. Typically, each stop will be a place the party can rest, recuperate, and refresh themselves before the next destination. 

&nbsp; 

Once players have established their Legs, the GM will assign each a travel distance, which is tracked using a Clock, and create the appropriate Journey Decks. From there, players will play through travel days, making progress to their destination, finding discoveries, and dealing with every obstacle that comes in their way. At the start of each day, the GM will draw from the **Event Deck** for the current Leg to determine the day's weather and any major events, such as roaming spirits or other travelers.

&nbsp;

From there, the GM will lay out the days' Stakes, and the players will make their Travel Roll, play through the **Menace Deck** (with increased effect if not uncovered) and anything uncovered from the **Exploration Deck**, then make camp for the night, rest, and prepare for the next day. Characters continue to make Travel Rolls until they have accumulated enough travel progress to fill the Clock, which indicates that they'll reach their goal on that day of travel. From there, the game plays as normal, with players overcoming the obstacles required to accomplish their goal or simply resting in a town to prepare for the next phase of travel. Once players reach their final goal and accomplish (or fail to accomplish) their task, the process begins again with the return journey to make it back home.
&nbsp;
The _Menace Deck_ represents issues the party can run into while traveling; some severe, and some quite mundane. Typically, for a relatively safe area, there should be one card per two characters, but as the area becomes more dangerous this increases. In addition to the dangers caused by the region, characters will add cards to the _Menace Deck_ in some situations:
* If a character has at least half their _Health Bar_ filled, add a _Minor Mishap_ to the _Menace Deck_. When drawn, it will always include them.
* If a character has at least half their _Health Bar_ filled with _Fatigue_ or _Wounds_, add a _Mishap_ to the _Menace Deck_. When drawn, it will always include them.
&nbsp;

### The Travel Roll
Each day when the party attempts to make progress to their destination they should make a Travel Roll, which is a specialized Skill Challenge. In general, characters don't get bonus dice for a travel roll except for abilities that specifically grant them. Most bonus dice will be granted by either knowledge of the route, such as experience or a detailed map, or a Talent that is granting a situational bonus.

&nbsp;

Like any Skill Challenge, the GM will first lay out the stakes, many of which are standardized for the Travel Roll, and players must decide if they are being **Bold** or **Cautious** in their travel. In almost every case, there will be one Automatic Stake, **Speed**, which grants a single segment on the progress clock_. In general, all characters make their Travel roll separately, although some Stakes may benefit the whole party or may be offset by a single character. In these cases, provide an additional stakes sheet that only has the **Dangers** and **Augments** for the whole party to simplify tracking whether those have been dealt with.

&nbsp;

While traveling, the following are common Stakes that the GM might use:

&nbsp;
**Dangers:**
* **Affliction:** In a dangerous areas with odd weather or terrain **Fatigue** or other afflictions might be a danger, although appropriate equipment may automatically remove those dangers automatically.
* **Delay:** In areas where navigation is difficult this Danger removes a segment from the Clock for the current Leg, after adding any **Speed** from the current one. A single character can offset this **Danger** for the whole party.
* **Expense:** In areas of extreme heat, characters must mark off a day of water of gain **Thirst**. Other, more exotic climates might require a different resource to navigate, such as antidotes in a toxic swamp.
* **Fatigue:** In particularly rough terrain characters might risk additional **Fatigue**.
* **Indiscretion:** This stake is usually present, and represents the party leaving a trail. Each party member needs to offset this individually.
* **Menace:** Any dice placed on this **Danger** can be spent to see a card in the **Menace Deck**. This represents characters scouting out dangers and finding risks before they happen. Sometimes this may allow the party to completely avoid a hazard but, more often, it will provide significant advantage when dealing with the problem.

&nbsp;

**Augments:**
* **Advantage:** You find a good site to camp for the night, lowering the burden for what equipment is required to get a sufficient rest.
* **Efficacy:** Choose a single Job to work on during this travel. Any die placed on this Job increase the effect. Many Talents provide additional jobs, but common ones are below:
    * **Aid:** You may give any die spent on this to other characters, who may immediately roll them and spend them on **Dangers**/**Augments** as appropriate.
    * **Chart:** Mark one segment on the Route Mastery Clock for this route. Only one character may choose this option per day, and only one segment may be placed on it.
    * **Forage:** Roll 2d6 and consult the **Forage Chart**, gaining that result or any lower than it on the table. For each additional die placed on this roll an additional die, resolving the effect with the highest 2.
    * **Hunt:** As **Forage**, but instead roll on the **Hunting Chart**. Characters may only select this option if they have some sort of ranged attack suitable for hunting (typically an Attack that is at least Range 2).
* **Observation:** Each die on this **Augment** allows the party to draw a single card from the **Exploration Deck**, resolving its effect.

&nbsp;

When traveling, the total progress marked is the lowest progress that any party member achieved. This means that all characters must play the **Speed** Advantage the same number of times; be flexible with allowing players to rearrange how they spent their dice to make sure nobody gets left behind.

&nbsp;
While traveling, the standards for Equipment providing a bonus are significantly higher. Most relevant Equipment will specify its bonuses, such as automatically removing a Danger related to a specific effect.
&nbsp;
Finally, characters may choose to have their spirit _Manifested_ for the majority of the travel day. If they are, they may have Traits or other abilities that allow them to assist the party, but will automatically take one _Fatigue_, with the potential for more if the climate does not suit them.

&nbsp;
### Making Camp
After a long day of travel, the party will typically make camp. When they do, the party has a chance to decompress, gather themselves, and rest. When resting, each party member can do a single **Camp Action**, and then makes their **Rest Roll**.

&nbsp;

**Camp Actions** are all small things that need to be done around the camp, or can help a character properly recover. Each character can normally do one per Camp Phase. In general, basic tasks such as breaking camp, making a fire, and setting up tents are done without a character using their Camp Action. Common Camp Actions are as follows:
* **Cook:** Much of the food brought on journeys doesn't requires cooking, but game caught on the trail or other similar foods must be cooked to be safely eaten. This action requires Cooking Tools and a fire.
* **Cover:** You take the time to hide traces of the camp, which may provide an Advantage.
* **Forage/Hunt:** As the options during Travel, but treat yourself as having placed 0 die on the effect, causing you to roll three dice and take the worst two.
* **Mend:** Choose a piece of Equipment that is missing _Durability_ but not _Destroyed_. If you have the appropriate tools, roll a d6. On a 4+, restore one _Durability_. Only one character can target the same item, and this requires the appropriate tools for working with that material.
* **Patch:** Mark off a medicine. You or another character roll an additional die on their _Rest Roll_, and may immediately mark one segment on a Wound Clock. A character may only benefit from this once per Camp.
* **Rest:** You and your Spirit gain an additional die on your _Rest Roll_.
* **Secure:** You take the time to fortify the camp, which may provide an Advantage.
* **Vent:** Choose another character; the two of you roleplay a quick scene; afterwards, both of you clear 1 **Stress** and gain a shared _Bond_. This requires both of your _Camp Actions_.
* **Channel:** You may only take this action if you have considerable _Source_ for your Spirit. Your spirit rolls an 2 die during its _Rest Roll_.
* **Pamper:** You spend time with your spirit and taking care of it, whether that be playing with it, styling it, or something similar. Afterwards, you or your spirit clears 1 _Stress_. If you have an appropriate item, repeat the effect.


&nbsp;

After finishing their Camp Action, characters will mark off one Water and one Food or take the appropriate conditions, then make a **Rest Roll** to see how well they're able to recover. Characters and their Spirit both make a _Rest Roll_. The **Rest Roll** typically has an automatic Stake of a single **Efficacy**, and often includes the following:

&nbsp;
**Dangers**
* **Affliction (Crash):** You and your spirit sleep far too heavily or are otherwise unaware, leaving you unresponsive to any dangers for the night.

&nbsp;
**Augments**
* **Efficacy:** Remove a **Fatigue** from yourself or mark one segment on a Wound Clock.

&nbsp;
If characters are unable to get a quality rest either due to not finding a sufficiently safe place to camp or missing essential equipment such as bedrolls or tents they take a 1-2 die penalty on their roll. Spirits, instead of needing appropriate sleeping equipment, typically gain or lose a die based on how aligned with the current environment they are. If conditions are sufficiently dire additional Dangers may be added. Characters always roll their **Rest Roll** **Cautiously**, unless specified otherwise.

### Travel Pace
All the above rules assume a normal travel pace. Characters may make the following changes to their Travel/Rest for the following effects:
* **Rest:** Skip the _Travel Roll_ today; you make no progress and remain in your camp. You may make your _Rest Roll_ _Boldly_, automatically benefit from the _Rest_ _Camp Action_ and may do two _Camp Actions_.
* **Explore:** Lose the automatic **Speed** stake, but roll +1 die on your **Travel Roll**.
* **Forced March:** Gain an additional automatic **Speed** stake. Take an additional **Fatigue**, and roll -1 die on your **Travel Roll**.
* **Double Shift:** While Making Camp, you may take an additional Camp Action. You may not do the **Rest** Camp Action tonight. Additionally, you lose the automatic **Efficacy** stake when resting.

### Gameplay Example
John, Margaret, Winona and Erika are all beginning their journey from Serpent Village to the Library, a long trip across the Greenvale. To start, the GM sets the overall distance for the Journey, and, after looking at the map, decides an 8 segment _Clock_ is appropriate. It's a long trip, and will likely take several days for the party to get there but the terrain is largely the same and, as such, the trip doesn't need to be split up into multiple Lgs.
&nbsp;
With that established, the GM gets the remainder of his resources together: the _Hunting_ and _Forage_ Charts for the _Greenvale_, as well as the _Herbalism Chart_ due to Erika's _Herbalist_ Talent. Additionally, the GM prepares a handful of events that will happen on each day of travel, as well as harmful events for the _Menace Deck_ and interesting discoveries for the _Exploration Deck_. With that preparation done, the GM and players begin the journey! To start, the GM describes the weather and climate of the first day; clear skies, as far as the eye can see.
&nbsp;
To start, the GM lets the players know that, due to the easy terrain of the Greenvale, everybody will be receiving a bonus die on their _Travel Roll_. Additionally, on the first day they'll get an additional bonus die due to being very familiar with the area around Serpent Village. The party isn't overly concerned with running into dangers on their first day of travel and decide that they will be traveling _Boldly_. The GM lays out the stakes, which are fairly standard; an automatic _Speed_ just for traveling, 1 _Fatigue_ for each person as well as an _Indiscretion_ stake. They also lay out 4 _Menace_ stakes to represent the dangers of the road here. The GM does not tell the players this, but the _Menace_ deck consists of 2 _Minor Mishaps_, a _Mishap_, and an encounter with a dangerous roaming earth spirit.
&nbsp;
With that, each player assembles their dice pool and rolls to see what resources they have for the day!
* Erika is traveling with a light load and gains a bonus die, allowing her to roll 5 _Boldly_. After an initial roll of 3 [-] and 2 [ ], Erika rerolls all her die, getting a final pool of 2 [-], 2 [ ], and 1 [+].
* John has no special skills to modify his dice pool and simply rolls 4 _Boldly_. After an initial roll of 2 [ ] and 2 [+], John rerolls his [ ], getting a final pool of 1 [ ] and 3 [+].
* Margaret, due to her _Traveler_ ability, gets an additional die, bringing her total to 5. Additionally, she can choose a different approach to the rest of the party and chooses to roll _Cautiously_ due to her _Traveling Heavy_ ability from high encumbrance (which she adds an additional _Fatigue_ for). She rolls her die, and initially gets 4 [-] and 1 [ ]. After rerolling her lone [ ] she ends with the same total.
* Winona is also traveling light and rolls 5 _Boldly_. After an initial roll of 1 [-] and 1 [ ] and 3 [+], Winona rerolls her [-] and [ ], getting a final pool of 1 [-] and 4 [+].
&nbsp;

&nbsp;
With their die pools created, the party begins their day traveling and starts to strategize on how to spend their dice. Everybody has at least one [+] except Margaret, so Winona decides to use the <u>Quick March</u> ability from _Swift_ to allow Margaret to spend her [ ] for _Speed_ and causing Winona a _Fatigue_ as she helps her slower friend keep up. After that, the others all spend 1 [+] for _Speed_, allowing them to make more progress towards their destination. Because Erika only has a single [+] to spend on _Speed_ and Margaret has no more remaining, it doesn't make sense for any other party members to spend die on _Speed_ as the progress would not count without the whole party.
&nbsp;
The GM begins to narrate their swift progress towards their goal, as well as a brief encounter they have with some other travelers who end up being friendly. The party doesn't have any great trade goods on them, and Winona asks if she could have spent [+] on hunting prior to this, which the GM agrees to. Due to her _Hunter_ Talent Winona treats herself as having placed an additional die on the Augment, so chooses to only spend 1 [+]. She rolls 3 die and gets a 10 after taking the highest two which, in the Greenvale, corresponds to large birds and some high quality feathers, which she uses the <u>Efficient Butcher</u> part to get more of. Even though Winona gained a large amount of _Load_ from this, we don't adjust any of the die rolls until the next day of travel. Winona decides she doesn't want to trade away the meat but doesn't need the feathers, and is able to get some basic supplies in exchange for them.
&nbsp;
With that done, Margaret decides that she's spending a great deal of the day scouting out dangers and spends 3 of her [-] on _Menace_, spending the last one one of her two _Fatigue_. With that she draws from the deck and is able to remove one of the _Minor Mishap_s, the _Mishap_, and the spirit encounter. For the first two, the GM narrates a short scene where Margaret caught an argument brewing between two characters and diffused it before it could escalate, as well as her catching Erika when she slipped on uneven ground, keeping her from tumbling into small gultch. 
&nbsp;
For the roaming earth spirit, the GM instead narrates that Margaret is able to notice it coming while it's still a ways out, and asks the party what they want to do. They would like to avoid combat this early into the Journey, and decide to hide in some trees until the spirit passes, with Erika's spirit luring it away. The GM decides this is a reasonable plan and, after a very simple skill challenge for Erika's spirit, the party is able to accomplish this without issue, although the spirit takes a _Stress_ from not being able to remove a _Danger_ in the Skill Challenge.
&nbsp;
With that handled, the rest of the party figure out how to allocate their die. Winona removes her _Fatigue_ using her lone [-], then (due to her _Ranger_ Talent allowing her to do multiple jobs) decides to spend her last two [+] on the <u>Forage</u> job. After rolling three times and taking the best two she only gets a 6, giving her another 2 _Food_ and ensuring the party has enough water and firewood.
&nbsp;
Erika decides to use her [-] to remove her _Fatigue_ as well as the last _Menace_, and the GM narrates another short scene where she diffuses a charged situation in the party. After that she has no die remaining, as her [ ] can't be spent on anything.
&nbsp;
Finally, John spends his remaining two [+] on _Observation_, as he wants to explore the area. After doing so, he draws twice from the _Exploration Deck_. The first discovery is the site of a fight that happened recently. The party decides to investigate, and finds clear signs of spiritcraft. From the looks of the battlefield it seems this was a battle between people, as the damage to the surroundings isn't consistent with the damage to the area. With Winona's skill as a hunter she's able to accurately determine where the winners left to, as the trail is obvious. The party briefly deliberates on what to do, and decides that, for now, they won't pursue as they're in a hurry. John's second draw from the _Exploration Deck_ is more immediately helpful; along the road they find an old shrine to a spirit that hadn't been properly maintained. While there they find supplies, likely from a Keeper that was there. Much of it is rotted away, but a small notebook seems to have survived the ravages of time, as well as a _Health Flask_. Unfortunately, nobody in the party can read, so the book is simply packed away for now. Finally, without any [-] he is forced to simply take the _Fatigue_.
&nbsp;
Before ending travel for the day, both John and Margaret have boots that can aid in their travel. Both roll; John's ability does not help, but Margaret's _Travelers Boots_ remove a _Fatigue_ stake for her.
&nbsp;
At the end of the day of travel, the following has changed for each party member:
* Margaret: No change!
* Winona: Gained 1 _Fatigue_, but also found 4 Good _Food_ [_Meat_] and 2 Basic _Food_.
* Erika: Vicki and Belle (Erika's spirit) gained 1 _Stress_.
* John: Gained 1 _Fatigue_!
&nbsp;

&nbsp;
With the day's journey done, the party finds a decent place to make camp and begins to settle in for the night. Each member of the party decides on what they want to do with the night; Winona, with her _Cooks Tools_, decides to cook the meat she found today before it goes bad, and takes the <u>Cook</u> _Camp Action_. Because the meat was high quality everybody has a chance to clear a _Stress_ but, fortunately, nobody in the party is _Stressed_. Due to her foraging water and firewood are handled, which also helps.
&nbsp;
John has his spirit Kit use his _Metallic Threads_ ability to automatically <u>Secure</u> the camp, then decides to just take the <u>Rest</u> Camp Action.
&nbsp;
Erika didn't have the chance to forage for herbs today but Vicki and Belle were stressed by their encounter with the earth spirit, so she takes the time to _Pamper_ her spirit, removing its _Stress_.
&nbsp;
Finally, Margaret decides to take the <u>Cover</u> camp action; while the Greenvale is safe, they had literally just seen signs of combat, and making their camp a little harder to find is not a bad idea.
&nbsp;
With that done, everybody settles in for the night and makes their camp rolls. They have bedrolls, tents, and more than sufficient supplies to be comfortable and incur no penalties. Everybody is rolling only two die, except John and Kit, who get to roll 3 because they took the <u>Rest</u> _Camp Action_.
&nbsp;
Rest rolls are always rolled _Cautiously_, and each party member and their spirit rolls.
* Erika, Vicki, and Belle both end with 1 [-] and 1 [ ], which is enough to remove the <u>Crash</u> _Affliction_ on both.
* John gets 2 [-] and 1 [+], and decides to remove his _Fatigue_ and the <u>Crash</u> _Affliction_. Kit gets 1 [-] and 2 [ ], and simply removes the <u>Crash</u> _Affliction_.
* Margaret and Queen both get at least 1 [-] and also remove the <u>Crash</u> _Affliction_; they have nothing to spend further die on, and they are lost.
* Finally, Winona gets a final roll of 1 [-] and 1 [+], allowing her to remove the <u>Crash</u> _Affliction_ and her _Fatigue_. Her spirit, Pepper, rolls poorly and is unable to remove the <u>Crash</u> _Affliction_.
&nbsp;

&nbsp;
The party sleeps soundly through the night and, after a day of adventure, everybody has maintained a clear _Health Bar_ and they've made good progress towards their destination. However, the following morning while they're breaking camp Winona notices tracks nearby that match some seen at the battlefield they found; it seems they were followed, although they weren't able to locate their camp. Additionally, now that they are further from Serpent Village they'll all roll one less die, ensuring the rest of this journey is a bit more difficult.
&nbsp;
As a general note, normally a party traveling _Boldly_ would end the day with more Conditions, but due to great rolls on Margaret's part, the bonus die the party received, and the generally low danger of the Greenvale they were able to successfully navigate without incident.
