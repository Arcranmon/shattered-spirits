### The Greenvale

Named as such for its lush greenery and relative safety within the Shattered World, Greenvale is the closest things to a peaceful region that can be found. Located on a wide peninsula and cut off from the rest of the continent by a mountain range that seems to reach the sky, the Greenvale is spared the worst of rampaging spirits.
&nbsp;
That is not, to say, that the Greenvale is without its dangers. Thick forests cover much of the peninsula, and the creatures within do not take kindly to wanderers. The plenty of the land has lead to more settlements than other regions and, with them, conflict between the various groups. A true pillaging or invasion would risk the wrath of a town's Guardian, but homesteaders and others that live outside the proper bounds of a village must remain ever vigilant not only against the threat of other spirits, but also raiders from the less scrupulous villages.
&nbsp;
### Major Settlements of the Greenvale
##### Serpent Village
The largest and (allegedly) oldest village within the Greenvale, Serpent Village has the blessing of having an incredibly easy to deal with guardian. The titular serpent is a gentle, sleeping giant; at first, much of the town was built within the bounds of its great coils but, as the years have gone on, more and more construction is built on top of the snake itself, using its great body as a form of natural scaffolding. Within the town, a great deal of work goes into keeping the serpent happy and sleeping. Vast channels have been carved to ensure the town can keep the snake perpetually warm, as if it become cold it might shift in an attempt to find better sunlight. The areas near the head of the snake have strict rules for keeping quiet and, as an emergency measure, the town keeps vast supplies of dreamweed available.
&nbsp;
The shelter and stability of the serpent has lead to one of the more agrarian and urban villages within the Shattered World; while there is effort around keeping the serpent calm, it isn't nearly on the same level as most guardians. This has allowed Serpent Village to flourish with trade and craftspeople, and enshrining it as the central hub of the Greenvale.
&nbsp;
The only downside of Serpent Village is that, while the serpent's presence is enough to keep hostile spirits at bay, other creatures are much more willing to attempt something against the town as there is little to no risk of reprisal from the spirit. Fortunately, the serpent is a sort of natural wall, allowing the town to repel these assaults.
&nbsp;
##### Phoenix Village
The second largest village in the Greenvale, Phoenix Village lies near the base of the Coiled Mountains. Their spirit, the Monkey-Faced Phoenix, is smaller in stature than most guardians but makes up for it in power and raw ferocity. Unlike most villages, the rituals for Phoenix Village are well-known: the Monkey-Faced Phoenix craves bloodsport. A great arena dominates the center of the town, and the spirit is kept entertained by frequent bouts, always to the death. If both warriors fight well and with honor, the Monkey-Faced Phoenix will restore them to life to fight another day. Travelers are often forced to participate in battles in exchange for shelter in the town, stretching the limits of what might be considered hospitality.
&nbsp;
Likely due to the rituals required by their spirit, Phoenix Village is incredibly warlike and martially adept. Most conflicts are settled through ritual combat, either by the participants or by proxies in extreme cases. Most everyone within the town is a capable warrior and, even without their guardian spirit, it's unlikely any would be foolish enough to attack the village. Nearly everything within the village revolves around enhancing ones combat abilities, and much of Phoenix Village's 'trade' is effectively mercenary work in exchange for supplies.
&nbsp;
##### Black Harbor
The most prominent port in the Greenvale, Black Harbor gets its name for the inky black color of waves and the tentacles of their spirit, Arms Dance Like Waves. Unlike Phoenix Village, whatever the ritual Black Harbor does to placate their spirit has been kept a closely-guarded secret, although many of their sailors will disappear for days on end and return with little to show for it, creating a popular theory that whatever is done, it is done deep at sea.
&nbsp;
The most prominent industry within Black Harbor is their ship-making and sailing; if you need a boat in the Greenvale, Black Harbor is the only place you might be able to find safe passage. This has lead to them becoming a booming trade town, bolstered by the wide-variety of different inks they can source from local squids. These squids largely produce black ink but, on occasion, squids that produce other colors inks are born and carefully raised by the town. The dyemasters in the town are experts of watercraft, and have invented techniques that use immense precision and skill for intricate dye patterns that should not be possible.
&nbsp;
With their access to so many goods and the inks produced by their squids, Black Harbor is a vibrant, colorful place. The people of the town are heavily tattooed, are almost constantly smiling, and adorned in finery that people from other villages can only dream of.
&nbsp;
##### Library
Situated entirely underground, Library is sometimes referred to as the gateway to the Underworld. Built almost entirely within a (relatively) safe portion of the Underworld, Library is a wealth of old world resources. Ancient relics, odd devices, and high-quality metal abound in Library but nothing is quite so important as the wealth of tomes they have. While these are largely from the old world and their condition leaves something to be desired, Library has expanded its collection greatly, and is now the intellectual hub of the Greenvale.
&nbsp;
Library's spirit is a metal spirit, not much larger than a man and approximating the appearance of an owl. The townsfolk have affectionately named him Tock, as the owl seems to appreciate nothing more than the timekeeping devices of the old world, the gears of which he uses to adorn his body. Most assume the town's ritual has something with supplying Tock with old world devices, although others argue that doesn't feel sufficient to keep a spirit of his caliber satisfied.
&nbsp;
Within Library, inventors, scholars, and other similar professions abound. While the Underworld is normally inhospitable, the denizens of Library have found a way to carve a space out for themselves, rerouting ancient aqueducts, cultivating subterranean crops, and restoring enough functionality to old world devices to make them usable. In general, the residents of Library have spirits that trend on the smaller side; many travelers have experienced that larger spirits seem incredibly uncomfortable in Library, likely due to it being subterranean.
&nbsp; 
##### The Rainchasers
A nomadic tribe in the Greenvale, the Rainchasers make their journey every year from the sea to the base of the Coiled Mountains, following the trail of their spirit, Rising Waters. She takes the form of a great, multi-colored bird and, each year as the winter begins, roosts in the Coiled Mountains. When summer begins the snowmelt coming off the mountain carries her clutch of eggs, which the Rainchasers carefully carry to the sea where they may hatch, and then the journey begins anew.
&nbsp;
As travelers, none know the going-ons of the Greenvale as well as the Rainchasers, and they are always a welcome guest. They bring news of the other towns, always have merchandise to trade and, having much less protection against roaming spirits than the others, are extremely adept at craft and defending themselves. 
&nbsp;
The Rainchasers have heavily adopted the aesthetic of their spirit, and all collect a great feather from Rising Waters when they reach adulthood, which is always integrated into their clothing. They have cultivated a small herd of alpaca touched by water spirits, heightening the waterproof nature of their wool. This wool is used for almost all clothing among the Rainchasers, and garments made of it are highly sought after. Every summer, shortly after completing the year's journey, the Rainchasers always make a detour to Black Harbor to dye the year's wool in vibrant colors to match Rising Waters' plumage.
