#### Character Creation
##### Choosing Your Element
The first decision you make when creating your character within the Shattered World is choosing your element between Earth, Flame, Metal, Water, Wind, and Wood. The element you choose will decide what form your spirit takes, as well as what spiritcraft you have access to. You can always manipulate your element, but each also carries a unique physical, emotional, and sensory expression of that element.
&nbsp;
##### Choosing Your Discipline
Next, choose which subset of your element's magic you are most adept at. Each element carries with it five distinct forms of spiritcraft. Among the five unique types of craft, choose one that your character excels at. You gain that spiritcraft at its Novice rank, and all other Disciplines at the Basic rank. Additionally, gain the Basic version of the appropriate Spirit Art for sensing your element.
&nbsp;
##### Choosing Your Archetype
Next up is your character's Archetype, effectively a single adjective to describe their defining trait. Whether your character is _Strong_, _Clever_, or _Swift_, your character will be at their best when acting in accordance with it.
&nbsp;
##### Choosing Your Careers
Next, you choose two Careers that represent what, besides your craft, you bring to your village. These provide your character much of their capability outside a battle; a _Farmer_ will have a vastly different set of skills than a _Hunter_ might, for example. If no careers match exactly what you want your character to do, look instead at the skills provided; you may be able to slightly tweak the flavor of an existing career to better align with your vision of your character.
&nbsp;
You might notice that there are no careers like Guard or Soldier; because the Shattered World is so dangerous, everybody is trained to fight. Moreover, as a player character you are expected to go on grand adventures and face down the most dangerous creatures in the world; being able to fight isn't a special capability, it's a prerequisite.
&nbsp;
##### Create Your Spirit
Once all the previous steps are complete, begin the process of creating your spirit. Your element is already selected, so you have only a handful of decisions to make for your spirit.
&nbsp;
First, choose your spirit's size between Tiny, Small, Medium, and Large. The larger a spirit you have the more powerful and sturdy it will be, while smaller spirits can be more easily manifested and possess greater agility.
&nbsp;
Once that's selected you may spend 6 _Trait Points_ on your spirit, distributed however you wish between specialized traits that only your element may purchase and more general traits that any creature may have. You can purchase specific animalistic traits, such as fangs, claws, or a shell, or more ephemeral things, such as making your spirit cute or ensuring it has a strong protective instinct. As you purchase traits, consider what kind of animal your spirit is! It doesn't need to be a real creature, and you are strongly encouraged to combine multiple animals or choose mythological creatures to make a truly unique creature.

##### Finalizing Your Character
Once the mechanics are complete, work on the finishing touches for your character, starting with their (and their spirit's) name. If you are struggling to come up with a name for your spirit, keep in mind that most people get their spirit slightly before puberty, so overly literal or slightly cringey names are extremely common. Come up with personalities for both you and your spirit, their likes and dislikes, and how the relationship between the two looks.
&nbsp;
Following that, work with the other players to figure out how the party fits together. Try to come up with strong connections with at least two other players to give the party a sense of cohesion and comradery.
&nbsp;
Finally, work with the GM to figure out how your character fits into their town, what their role is, and other details to enmesh you into the world. Come up with short and long-term goals for your character. Your character should want to adventure and should be able to work in a group.
&nbsp;
#### Progression
##### Abilities Per Level
1. Character Creation (see above)
2. +1 Discipline, +1 Martial Art, +1 Spirit Art
3. +1 Career, +1 Martial Art, +1 Spirit Art
4. +1 Discipline, +1 Martial Art, +1 Spirit Art
5. +1 Archetype, +1 Martial Art, +1 Spirit Art
6. +1 Discipline, +1 Martial Art, +1 Spirit Art
7. +1 Career, +1 Martial Art, +1 Spirit Art
8. +1 Discipline, +1 Martial Art, +1 Spirit Art
9. +1 Career, +1 Martial Art, +1 Spirit Art
10. +1 Archetype, +1 Discipline, +1 Martial Art, +1 Spirit Art
&nbsp;
##### Leveling Up
Typically, characters gain a level at the end of a journey but, in particularly long journeys, may level up at a significant milestone along the way.
&nbsp;
#### Retraining
In general, characters should be given a great amount of leeway in retraining abilities between journeys. Characters should be able to effectively rebuild their character after their first adventure if they're unhappy with any component of it, including their element. So long as players aren't abusing retraining to always have the most optimal abilities for the next adventure, retraining is assumed to always be allowed.
