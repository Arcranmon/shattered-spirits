### Combat in the Shattered World
Most issues in the Shattered World can be handled with simple skill challenges, including mundane, routine, or uninteresting combat. Tactical combat should only be entered when there is an interesting conflict, goals and secondary objectives that are more complicated than defeating the enemy, and the players have a real success of failure, if not defeat.
&nbsp;
### Rounds in Shattered Spirits
Combat in Shattered Spirits is split up into _Rounds_, _Phases_, and _Turns_.

&nbsp;
A _Round_ is a complete cycle of combat, where each _Phase_ is completed.

&nbsp;
A _Phase_ is a collection of _Turns_ that are all occurring at a similar time within the _Round_.

&nbsp;
A _Turn_ is a specific character's opportunity to act in combat, where they can use various _Techniques_ to influence the battle.

&nbsp;
A _Round_ is structured as follows:
&nbsp;
**Respite:** The start of each _Round_. This is not a _Phase_, and no Abilities are used. During Respite, resolve the following in the following order:
* Lose all your current _Momentum_, gaining 2 _Guard_ for every _Momentum_ lost in this way.
* Lose all _Guard_ and _Movement_.
* Change your current _Stance_, if desired.
* You draw energy from your surroundings, powering your spiritcraft:
    * If the current location is not suppressing your Spiritcraft, gain 1 _Craft Momentum_.
    * If the current location is particularly well-suited for your Spiritcraft, gain 1 _Craft Momentum_.
    * If you are at a _Place of Power_, gain 1 _Craft Momentum_.
    * If your _Spirit_ is at a _Place of Power_, gain 1 _Craft Momentum_.
* Choose a _Stance_ for your _Spirit_, then, if the Stance is not _Spiritual Essence_, you may _Manifest_ your Spirit at an appropriate _Source_ (as defined by their size) within their _Manifest Range_. You may always _Manifest_ them at _Melee_ Range regardless of _Source_ for 1 _Momentum_. They become _Engaged_ with any creatures at said _Source_.
* You and your Spirit gain _Momentum_, _Movement_, and _Guard_ as outlined by your _Stance_s and other abilities.
* If applicable, you may _Recover_ once.
* Finally, you may immediately use a single [_Armament_] _Maneuver_, paying all costs as normal.

&nbsp;
**Phase 1-6:** These numbered Phases show the passing of time in combat and are when most action will take place. Each character's _Stance_ has a listed Speed, which is the _Phase_ that character takes their _Turn_ in. Any _Summoned_ Spirits also act at their listed _Speed_. If, for some reason, a character would end up acting in a Phase earlier than 1 or later than 6, they instead gain or lose respective _Momentum_ equal to the difference.

&nbsp; 
**End of Round:** The end of the Round. Many effects resolve in this window.

&nbsp;
An individual _Phase_ is divided into _Turns_. Within a _Phase_, all players go, then all enemies for that _Phase_.

&nbsp;

### Turns in Shattered Spirits
On **their** _Turn_, a character may do the following:
* Spend any amount of _Movement_.
* Use any number of _Abilities_. A given _Ability_ may only be used once per _Turn_ unless it has the _Repeatable_ keyword.
* Make a single _Attack_. Some _Stances_ remove this limit.

&nbsp;
Additionally, on **all** _Turns_ (including their own), creatures may do the following:
* Use any number of _Reactions_. A given _Reaction_ can only be used once per _Turn_ unless specified otherwise, and only one _Reaction_ may be used against a specific trigger.
* Use any number of _Gambits_, when applicable.

&nbsp;
Spirit Turns work differently than player ones. A Spirit's _Stance_ will dictate what it is going to do on its turn. For example, _Skirmisher Spirit_ requires that the Spirit attempt to _Engage_ an enemy, then Attack them. In most cases Spirits may use any abilities that make it safer or easier to accomplish this task, but players must, to the best of their abilities, always attempt to fulfill the objective of their Spirit's Stance. For example, if the Stance dictated that a Spirit needs to get to a location and the only way for the Spirit to get there would create an [_Opening_] and they do not have sufficient _Movement_ to prevent it, the Spirit must create said [_Opening_].

&nbsp;
A Spirit may always use _Guarder_ and _Spiritcraft_, regardless of their current _Stance_. However, they may not use them until completing the normal requirements of their _Stance_.

&nbsp;
**Advantage and Disadvantage:** Sometimes Attacks will have _Advantage_ or _Disadvantage_. An Attack with either rolls 3d6 instead of the normal 2d6. If the Attack has _Advantage_ the attacker resolves the attack with two dice of their choice (typically the highest), while _Disadvantage_ uses the lowest two rolls. If an Attack has both _Advantage_ and _Disadvantage_ they offset one another at a 1-to-1 rate. If an Attack has multiple instances of _Advantage_/_Disadvantage_ any additional instances are instead converted to a _Bonus_/_Penalty_.
&nbsp;

**Calculating Damage:** When determining how much damage is dealt to a creature, use the following order:
1. Determine the base Damage, as defined by the Attack or other effect.
2. Apply _Weakness_ or _Resistance_.
3. Add any modifiers from current Status Effects.
4. Apply _Ward_.
5. Take any inflicted _Stun_.
6. Apply _Block_.
7. Take any remaining Damage to _Stamina_.

&nbsp;
**Resolving Attacks:** When resolving the effects of an Attack, deal all Damage prior to resolving any Status. Additionally, the Target of an Attack should be aware of the Damage and Status that would be inflicted prior to using Reactions.

&nbsp;

**Extreme Rolls:** There are a variety of Reactions and other effects that can influence Attack Rolls, such as the Deflect Basic Skill, which reduces an _Attack Roll_ by 1. Whenever these are applied, resolve them using the modified _Attack Roll_. If an _Attack Roll_ ever ends up above a 12 or below a 2, instead resolve it as a 12 or a 2 respectively.

&nbsp;
**Range:** Attacks in Shattered Spirits will have a Range of _Self_, _Melee_, or a number between 0 and 3. 
* Self-Range effects either only affect the user. 
* Melee effects may only target creatures you are _Engaged_ with.
* Other effects may target any creature within that many Arenas of you, with 0 Range allowing you to target within your Arena, and 3 allowing you to attack up to 3 Arenas away.

&nbsp;
### Resources in Combat

* **Stamina:** A representation of a character's stamina and vitality. When this bar is emptied, the character gains a _Wound_.
* **Guard:** A representation of a character's ability to defend themself. Characters may choose to lose _Guard_ instead of _Stamina_ when they take Damage, up to their _Block_ value.
* **Momentum:** The resource used to fuel most a character's abilities and attacks. This is used for almost all of a character's abilities, and is roughly an approximation of how much they can do on their turn. All Momentum is lost at the start of the _Round_, so characters are encouraged to use it.
* **Posture:** 
* **Craft Momentum:** Craft Momentum is almost identical to Momentum, and represents a character's stores of spiritual energy and ability to channel Momentum into craft. Characters can typically convert small amounts of Momentum into Craft Momentum, and also receive some for free based on the environment. However, there are three key differences between the Momentum types:
    * First, Craft Momentum can be used by either a character or their Spirit.
    * Second, Craft Momentum may only be used on abilities with the [_Spirit_] keyword.
    * Finally, Craft Momentum is not lost at the start of a Round.
* **Movement:** A resource representing how mobile a character is, and how much they can move about the battlefield. Characters may always spend _Movement_ in the following ways on their _Turn_:
    * 1 _Movement_: If not _Engaged_ with a hostile creature, become _Engaged_ with a creature in your _Arena_.
    * 1 _Movement_: Remove _Engaged_.
    * 2 _Movement_: If not _Engaged_ with a hostile creature, move to an adjacent _Arena_. This cost is often modified by features of either _Arena_, and can be higher or lower.
    * 1 _Movement_: If not _Engaged_ with a hostile creature, _Occupy_ _Terrain_ or a _Hazard_. You automatically become _Engaged_ with any other creatures _Occupying_ the same effect.
    * 1 _Movement_: If not _Engaged_ with a hostile creature, take a specific position within your _Arena_, the effects of which should be resolved with your GM. For example, you might move to block a piece of unattended Equipment, which would require that any creature that wants to grab it become _Engaged_ with you.

Arenas are the basic layout of the combat map, and represent distinct areas in a battlefield, with your typical combat consisting of 6-10 Arenas. Each Arena typically represents a distinct and prominent terrain feature (such as the **Steep Cliffs** or **Underground River** on the above map) or is simply used to demonstrate a large amount of distance (such as splitting the **Cave Entrance** and the **Cave Center**). Characters may spend 1 _Movement_ to move to an adjacent _Arena_.

&nbsp;
 On the above map, the **Cave Entrance**, **Underground River**, **Side Tunnel Entrance**, **Cave Center**, **Steep Cliffs**, **High Ground**, **Egg Storage**, **Narrow Tunnel**, and **Queen's Lair** are the relevant Arenas, each with their own distinct effects, as outlined below.

 * The **Cave Entrance** is a simple arena. Characters in this Arena can spend 1 _Movement_ on their _Turn_ to flee the battlefield. Additionally, there are two large _Stalagmite_s that characters can _Occupy_ for _Cover_.
 * The **Cave Center** is also simple, with two large _Stalagmite_s that characters can _Occupy_ for _Cover_. It also has two _Stalactite_s that can be can attacked as if they were in Arena above the center; if they take at least 10 Damage they fall; all creatures in the Arena must make _Negate_ [_Reflex_ 10] taking 10 Damage on a failure or half on a success. Finally, creatures may pay 1 extra _Movement_ (total of 3) to move from the **Cave Center** to the **Side Tunnel Entrance** without Swimming or going into the _Underground River_.
 * The **Steep Cliffs** is a sheer wall that divides the elevations of the battlefield and counts as the _Cliff_ Terrain. It costs an additional two _Movement_ to enter and exit this _Arena_, and requires two _Free Hand_s. Additionally, while in this _Arena_, characters must use one Hand to maintain their hold, have _Disadvantage_ on all rolls, and all rolls against them have _Advantage_. Creatures with the _Climber_ trait ignore these penalties.
 * The **High Ground** is an area of the battlefield that overlooks the rest. Creatures on the **High Ground** have a +1 _Bonus_ to attack creatures in lower Arenas. Creatures that are Pushed from the **High Ground** to the **Cave Center** or **Cave Entrance** immediately _Fall_ 6.
 * The **Underground River** is a slow-running river that cuts through the battlefield. Creatures must _Swim_ through this _Arena_ but, other than that, it has no other effects.
 * The **Side Tunnel Entrance** has a single _Stalagmite_, as well as a _Tunnel_; while the Tunnel exists, additional enemies can enter the battlefield.
 * **Egg Storage** consists of a large number of locust egg clutches, 6 of which are large enough to be treated as Obstacles. They have a single _Durability_ and can be destroyed; otherwise, each creates a single _Hatchling_ each round.
 * The **Narrow Tunnel** is a choke point between the **Egg Storage** and **Queen's Lair** and is _Cramped_. This means it has only a single _Engagement_ that all characters are a part of. Characters may leave the _Arena_ directly from this _Engagement_, but it costs an additional _Movement_ to do so. Additionally, any _Heavy_ Attack made in this _Arena_ has _Disadvantage_.
 * The **Queen's Lair** is the final location within the battlefield, and has no special effects.

### Wounds and Health
Characters have several resources that represent their overall state in combat.
* <ins>Guard</ins> is a character's first line of defense, and is their ability to absorb or avoid incoming assaults without harm. Characters typically lose Guard by choice; when they take Damage, characters may lose Guard equal to their Block value in place of Stamina. If a character has no Guard remaining they cannot do this, and instead must take all Damage to Stamina. Guard is easily recovered with a variety of abilities, and is constantly being gained and lost.
* <ins>Stamina</ins> is a character's last line of defense and represents their ability to avoid major injury. When a character loses Stamina they are being pushed back, barely deflecting an attack, or taking superficial damage; they aren't taking true damage, but are also clearly on their back foot. When a character loses all their _Stamina_ they take a _Wound_, reset their Stamina to full, then take any remaining Damage. Characters typically start combat encounters with full _Stamina_.
* <ins>Downed</ins> occurs when a creature's _Stamina_ reaches 0. For most NPCs, this means they are immediately _Defeated_. Player characters and more powerful NPCs instead roll for _Wounds_. Characters roll 2d6 and subtract their current number of Wounds, then gain a _Wound_ as dictated by the damaging effect. After gaining _Wounds_ a character is reset to full _Stamina_, then takes any overflow damage.
* <ins>Spirit Downed</ins> occurs when a character's spirit's _Stamina_ reaches 0. When a _Spirit_ is _Downed_ that character becomes _Suppressed_ for the remainder of the _Round_ (or _Sealed_ if they are already _Suppressed_). Additionally, the spirit rolls for _Wounds_, using the same rules as a player. After gaining _Wounds_ the _Spirit_ is reset to full _Stamina_, then takes any overflow damage.
* <ins>Death</ins>: It is extremely rare for a character to be killed in combat in Shattered Spirits. Characters only die when their entire Health Bar is already full of _Wounds_ and they would gain another. If a character would gain a _Wound_ with more segments then they have available on their _Health Bar_ they fill everything they can, but do not die from said _Wound_; instead, the Clock to health is extended.

### Open and Hidden Information
**Open Information**: Players should <ins>always</ins> have access to the following information, either openly or by asking the Spirit Master:
* The victory condition for the current encounter.
* The approximate state of a character's Stamina, Guard, and Posture:
    * A character is either out of posture (0 _Posture_) or not (>0 Posture).
    * A character either has their guard up (>50% Guard remaining), their guard is faltering (<=50% guard remaining), or is defenseless (0 Guard).
    * A character is either fine (>50% Stamina) or on their back foot (<=50% Stamina), or some similar phrase.
* What _Statuses_ and _Afflictions_ a creature is currently suffering from.
* When/how any _Hazards_ on the battlefield will trigger, and the approximate consequence for doing so (i.e., 'you will be hit by an attack that might inflict _Shocked_').
* Once _Respite_ is complete, what _Phase_ all creatures are acting in.
&nbsp;

**Almost-Open Information:** Players should learn the following information after damaging a creature or applying an _Affliction_ or _Status_:
* If any damage they dealt was _Resisted_.
* The Target's _Block_.
&nbsp;

**Hidden Information:** Players should not know the following information unless an ability reveals it and, additionally, the GM should act as if they don't know the following about player characters unless an ability reveals it:
* A creature's exact, current amount of any resource.
* The creature's current _Stances_, and what benefits they have.

### Equipment in Combat
In combat, characters' equipment is defined by what they have _Worn_ and what they have _Wielded_.
&nbsp;

_Worn_ items are anything in a character's _Worn_ slots. A character's _Armor_, any consumable items, and any _Weapons_ stowed on their consumables are common examples of _Worn_ items.
&nbsp;

_Wielded_ items, on the other hand, are items that characters actively are using, typically by holding it in a hand. One-Handed items require a single hand to _Wield_, while Two-Handed items require both.
&nbsp;

If a character does not have an item _Wielded_ in a hand, that hand is considered an _Open Hand_.
&nbsp;

**Equipment and Open Hands:** In some cases Equipment must be _Wielded_ in two hands or gets additional benefits when _Wielded_ in such a way. In cases such as this, when a character is _Wielding_ a single piece of Equipment, they may choose on each _Turn_ if they are _Wielding_ it in one or both hands, but may not use it unless it is _Wielded_ in the appropriate number of hands.
&nbsp;

### Miscellaneous
* Always round up.
* You are not your own ally for the purposes of targeting.
* If the Cost of an ability is reduced to less than 0, any remaining reduction may (and should) be used on Enhancements.
* For _Status_:
    * When Statuses react with an effect, ignore all _Weakness_/_Resistance_ and similar from said Status when resolving the reaction.
    * A Statuses reaction may not trigger another of its own Reactions.
