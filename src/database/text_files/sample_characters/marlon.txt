### Marlon and Mora
*An enterprising merchant who is committed to someday making Serpent Village a trading destination and Mora, a snake-horse spirit made of water that really just wants to stop carrying merchandise.*
&nbsp;
A free spirit, Marlon spends more time out of Serpent Village than in it. He is constantly on the road with his spirit Mora, making a circuit between the different towns in the Greenvale to constantly trade for more and more interesting supplies. He is close with John, but his sense of style and ease with people seems to be a constant source of ire for Winona.
&nbsp;
Marlon has patches of iridescent scales across his body that constantly shimmer between different colors. While he claims that he only comes back to Serpent Village because it's stable and a good place to keep his things, he keeps a private journal where he's slowly plotting out his plans for a permanent storefront in Serpent Village when he builds up enough inventory.
&nbsp;
Between merchant journeys, Marlon makes quick adventures into the Underworld, finding new trinkets and other objects of interest. Originally, he had little actual interest in the Underworld and was mostly looking for more trinkets to barter with but, at this point, is one of the experts on the Underworld in Serpent Village.
&nbsp;
Mora, Marlon's spirit, is a large reptilian creature that is somewhere between a snake in a horse. She's covered in scales and has the general shape of a horse, but from the neck forward has a large snake-like neck and head. Despite her frightening appearance, she is a sweet, gentle spirit, and the largest danger she typically poses is a very wet hug. Mora loves lilly pads and, like Kit, hates birds.
